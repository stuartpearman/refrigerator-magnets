Suzannes Poems

this is a tribute to my sister Suzanne who left us too soon pick a poem to the right and make it your own by moving around the tiles

  • LA Dreams

    my feet never hurt at first. i spent my walks rehearsing how i would recount them someday when i “made it.” i thought, someday this will be the prologue to my cinderella story.

  • Brain to Brain

    how long will it be until there is no one left alive who appreciates books, the feel of them, the binding— until screens feel more natural than pages will we still read or will we just watch videos or holograms or only transfer thoughts from brain to brain

  • growing up

    when i was one, my parents called me “pumpkin.” when i was two, my sister was born, and i was pissed. when i was three, my sister became deaf and then later miraculously regained her hearing.

  • Fried Chicken

    we ate fried chicken me, my sister our friends while my parents drove my brother to the ER. the chicken felt stuck inside my throat and it was hard to swallow. i never ate fried chicken again.
