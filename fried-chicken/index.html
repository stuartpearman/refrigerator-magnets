i remember

my brother’s eye socket

filled with blood, a cascading

waterfall of red i couldn’t see through.

i couldn’t tell whether he had eyes still.

we ate fried chicken—me,

my sister, our friends—

while my parents drove my brother

to the ER. the chicken felt stuck

inside my throat and it was

hard to swallow.

i never ate fried chicken again.

my brother has both eyes still.

he was stitched back together,

but he still has

a scar, interrupting

the arch of his eyebrow.

—

my sister became deaf as a baby, when she had meningitis.

my parents took sign language classes,

and then, one day, her hearing returned.

—

my brother has eyes.

my sister has ears.

a miracle.

i think that i am the only member

of my family who thinks

about this often,

still.
